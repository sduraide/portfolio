Responsive Redesign

Introduction

I was browsing the "worst websites" on Google and came across Petersbuss, a Swedish travel agency website whose objective took a while for me to figure out. P.S language wasn't the barrier. To critic it professionally, I jotted down usability and accessbility issues to redesign with in mind before I started the design thinking process. I first made wireframes for different devices and then made hi-fi prototypes.

Chosen Website: Petersbuss

Problems with the website

  • Usability, Learnability, and Memorability

    1. There's no clear text hierarchy. Font size is sometimes too small.

    2. Home page is cluttered with a lot of unsegregated information making navigation hard.

    3. The objective of the website (CTA) is not clear.

    4. There are too many unnecessary colors.

    5. The images on the home page are low resolution and have distracting backgrounds.

  • Accessibility

    1. There isn't any defined language for screen reader.

    2. The images are missing alt texts due to which screen readers cannot interpret content correctly.

    3. The websites have a very low contrast.

    4. There aren't any page regions to provide navigation functionality to assistive technology users.

Potential Changes for the Project

    • While displaying problems, I'd segregate them into different usability problems for easier access.

    • In the prototype, I'd make annotations more distinct to avoid any confusion with the actual prototype.

    • I only chose to work on the some of the accessbility problems I found on WAVE. I want to learn how to effectively navigate accessibility issues head on.

Takeaways

  1. There's no way to determine which device the user will access your website from. In order to have a low bounce rate, responsiveness is vital.

  2. You might have to use different images for responsiveness.

  3. Keeping colors and text hierarchy plain and simple goes a long way.
