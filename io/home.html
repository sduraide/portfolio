Input/Output

Introduction

To design a user-driven website, designers are mostly in a situation to design a container for future inputs, rather than designing a layout that marries a specific form to content. In this project, I explore web as a platform for dynamic contents, focusing on how designing the container could influence the future contents.

Lofi Prototyping with Wireframes

I started off by brainstorming five different inputs and their respective outcomes through wireframes.

My mother is a strong believer in astrology based on the appearance of planets. I wondered how it would look like if the earth spun faster or if it was looked bigger to us. So for my layout, I wanted to have different inputs for bigger size and greater distance to see any planet by clicking on it.

When I first moved to the US, I was surprised how fast the sun would set during winter and how late it would set during summer. My surroundings changed because of that. So for my layout, I wanted to have a slider with the time increasing where they could see a place at different times of the day during a particular season like a time lapse.

When the pandemic broke out, I moved into my cousin's place. She has a five year old daughter who was obsessed with slimes. Playing with slimes would always calm her down. So for my layout, I wanted to allow users to pick a color for their slimes and drag it around with their mouse.

My professor for the Graphic Design class had taught us p5.js a couple days ago and I was inspired by this library that was artsy. So for my layout, I wanted users to be able to input the number of lines they want for the spiral, their opacity and width, and the rotation speed.

I am a huge Potter head and was rewatching the scene where the Fat Lady sings and shatters the glass. So for my layout, I wanted to create an effect from speech to text which involved recognizing the emotion through the voice, creating a background based on the emotion, and animating the text.

Initial Hifi Prototyping

After some feedback, I started contextualizing each input/output. I was struck by two ideas: the spiral art and the time lapse. I ended up making prototypes for each of them

The spiral art has a huge significance in my religion with engravings on multiple monuments. This was an interesting aspect for me to explore.

Since I'm far away from home almost the entire year, I would often wonder how it would be if I was at home right now and browse through my pictures.

Revised Hifi Prototyping

Since I had a more emotional connect to the time lapse prototype going to college in a different country, I decided to further that.

I chose popular cities in US as home, New York, Boston, and Philadelphia for the relative ease of high res images. I will be adding more cities as I go. Since I'm not from any of these cities, I asked my friends from NYC, Boston and Philly to see what locations in the city they considered home. I collected images and wrote captions based on this data.

I wanted users to have a more immersive experience and have a more personal feel compared to the initial prototype. Respectively, I added a bigger image with a gradient background matching the time and a night sky gazing background instead of a map since night is the time you usually feel homesick.

Initial Prototype with feedback

Final Website

After one more iteration, I figured out that nostalgia was my theme, instead of night sky gazing and portrayed that on the landing page by having an old paper texture as background and adding a film reel background to the cities. To take it to a further level of immersiveness, I followed a layout similar to Google Maps to have a full sized image with the option of VR viewing.

Potential changes to Website

Here are some of the changes I'd make with extra time and additional revision on my work.

    • Incorporate many more cities in different countries

    • Improve speed of loading for videos

    • Allow automatic transition of images along with the slider for user control

    • Adjust the 360 view to align with the current image in each city

Conclusion

In this project, I learned a lot about how to convey my mood in the form of webpages. The dynamic content for different inputs truly shapes the experiences of users. To have an immersive experience, the dimensions of an object can play a huge role. It also helps to explore similar websites once you have a fundamental idea for your layout.
