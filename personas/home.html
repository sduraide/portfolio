Personas and Storyboarding

Introduction

I bought a DSLR camera a few years ago out of peer pressure but I stopped using it because I didn't "find any time". I was curious to know the different type of users of the DSLR camera. In this project, I interviewed and observed different users interacting with the camera from which I created two different personas, and drew a storyboard based on one of the user's interaction with the interface.

DSLR Interface

A typical photography workshop covers the basics of DSLR in two weeks. Described below is a two minute version to have some basic knowledge to assist in personas and storyboarding.

Not a reader? A more interactive version helps you learn much more about the camera!

  • Lens is the eye of the camera. You rotate the end of the lens to determine focus
  • Focus is the area in the photo you want to be captured with best clarity
  • Shutter Button is the button to click to capture the photo
  • Shutter Speed is the time interval in which the shutter of lens shuts
  • Aperture and ISO help with the lighting of photos
  • Viewfinder is a small window to look through to compose the photo
  • Shooting Mode corresponds to the mode selected in Mode Dial
  • Mode dials determine the mode. For simplicity let's stick to Manual and Automatic
  • In manual mode, the photgrapher controls the aperture, shutter speed and ISO among others while in Automatic mode the camera determines it

Interviews and Observations

Statistics only give you "how" and "when"; users give you "why". To better understand the "why", I interviewed three different users about their lifestyle, expectations, motives and challenges about their camera, and observed how they interacted with the interface. For a more detailed interview, check this out!

    Lifestyle Question

  1. What do you enjoy? What do you do in your free time?
    • User 1 is a 20yr biotechnology student who loves to do nature photography. User 2 is a 40yr Noogler who is a casual photographer. User 3 is a 25yr old Architecture student who has a love-hate relationship with his camera.

    Expectation Questions

  2. Have you used/do you use a digital camera? What do you use the camera for?
    • User 1 has been obsessed with her camera since 8th grade and aims to take the perfect pictures. User 2 has been using a camera for a couple years after being inspired by a friend. It gives him a getaway from his “real life”. User 3 only uses his camera for course credit and is not a huge fan because of its size and weight.
  3. How would one (or you) define your relationship with your camera?
    • User 1 refers to her camera as her baby because of its monetary value and the fact that she can manipulate pictures to make it look perfect. User 2 finds his camera more reliable than a human being. User 3 has a conditional and flaky relationship with his camera because it has given him both good and bad times.

    Experience Questions

  4. What was it like to use the camera ? How did it feel?
    • User 1 feels satisfied, happy and creative while using the camera and brags about taking the best pictures. User 2 finds time to just fly past and also a little self conscious about his surroundings. User 3 initially found it hard to use it and loves capturing people’s, more importantly family’s, pictures and glancing back at them
  5. On a scale of 1 to 10, can you rate how successful you think you were?
    • User 1 usually rates her work 10/10, but gets very disappointed if she isn’t. User 2 doesn’t really judge success because he’s doing it for his happiness. User 3 ranks it as 4/10 as he gave up on it quickly after learning technicalities.

    Motive Question

  6. Do you expect to gain anything from using a DSLR?
    • User 1 would like some popularity and perfection. User 2 just wants something to be there for him. User 3 only likes the emotional aspect of pictures.

    Challenges Question

  7. If you had a magic wand, would you change something about the camera?
    • Everything about the camera is perfect for User 1 as she has the best camera, according to her. User 2 would like to normalize automatic mode and simplify the process. User 3 would like to reduce its size, weight and sensitiveness.

Personas & Empathy Maps

Following the interviews, I segregated the commonalities of users to form two different personas through empathy maps. A persona represents a type of a group of users of an interface. Empathy maps outline what this type of user says, does, thinks, and feels.

    User 1

  • User Description

  • A 20 year old biotechnology student who spends six hours a day with a camera for her page and recently adopted a kitten!

  • Why this persona?

    • Since she is a perfectionist, she would not be happy with herself until she captures the best photo, which leads to a high camera use.

    • She also wants to know everything there is about the camera and taking the "perfect picture" and is obsessed with it. And for all we know the perfect picture doesn't exist.

    • The positive stigma of photography also enhances her complex.

  • Problems with the interface

    • On one hand the user considers nothing as a difficulty. On the other hand would prefer a lighter camera, something more compact to be able to carry around especially when packing for vacations.

    • She also finds it hard to shoot moving objects because it takes a while for the camera to load when the settings are switched and by the time she gets the right settings, the perfect moment has passed. As she is a perfectionist, she gets disappointed because she couldn’t capture that moment.

    User 2

  • User Description

  • A 40 year Noogler whose hobby has been photography for the last 3 years. He was inspired by his friend's photography page on Instagram enabling him to have a getaway.

  • Why this persona?

    • Life is stressful and when it is, he picks up a camera to make himself happy. Who doesn’t want to be happy?

    • He wouldn't care if someone is better than him. He is doing it for himself and thus won't give up.

    • This is a rescue or life saver for him and he hangs on to it.

  • Problems with the interface

    • Dials pose a problem for this user. When he places it in the camera bag, the dials automatically move causing him to lose all the settings.

    • He finds it a hassle to delete pictures: one because it’s like erasing your good thoughts and two because his camera doesn’t allow him to delete multiple photos at once.

    • He also feels like there are too many settings to learn and wishes the process to be simpler.

Storyboard

Perfect Polly had an exciting way of taking her pictures so I made a storyboard based on her interaction with the DSLR. A Storyboard is a drawing that describes the series of events that occured in a particular incident.

Potential Improvements & Takeaways

    • I'd interview a wider range of people and segregate common answers instead of relying on individual answers. I'd also add more questions about the interviewee's knowledge about the camera to validate some of their answers.

    • It's interesting to observe that the same features in the DSLR camera are hugely beneficial to one user whereas they are absolutely useless and maybe even a pain for another user.

    • It's truly an art to write unbiased users since interviewee's are easy to nudge in a certain direction, consciously or unconsciously.

    • I recorded user's interactions with permission and I noticed many little things I missed out during real time.
